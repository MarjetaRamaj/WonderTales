Snowwhite and seven dwarfs


Once upon a time, in a land far away, there lived a beautiful princess named Snow White. She had skin as white as snow, lips as red as blood, and hair as black as ebony. Snow White's wicked stepmother, the Queen, was jealous of her beauty and ordered her huntsman to take her into the forest and kill her.



Feeling pity for Snow White, the huntsman let her go, warning her to flee into the woods and never return. Lost and frightened, Snow White stumbled upon a tiny cottage belonging to seven dwarfs. They welcomed her and allowed her to stay with them


Meanwhile, the Queen, believing Snow White to be dead, asked her magic mirror who was the fairest in the land. To her dismay, the mirror revealed that Snow White was still alive and the fairest of them all.


Determined to rid herself of Snow White once and for all, the Queen disguised herself as an old peddler woman and tricked Snow White into eating a poisoned apple. The poison caused Snow White to fall into a deep sleep that could only be broken by true love's kiss.


The dwarfs, unable to revive her, placed Snow White in a glass coffin. One day, a prince passing by saw Snow White and was captivated by her beauty. He kissed her, breaking the spell. Snow White awoke, and the prince declared his love for her. They were married, and Snow White became a princess once more.

The Queen, upon learning of Snow White's awakening, attended the wedding. But her jealousy and wickedness led to her downfall as she was punished for her evil deeds. Snow White and her prince lived happily ever after, ruling the kingdom with kindness and grace.
