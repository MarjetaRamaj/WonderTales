The little mermaid


Once upon a time, in the depths of the sea, lived a young mermaid named Ariel. She was the youngest daughter of the Sea King and had a beautiful, enchanting voice. Ariel was fascinated by the human world and collected many human artifacts, dreaming of living on land.



One day, Ariel saved a handsome prince from drowning during a storm and fell in love with him. Desperate to become human and be with the prince, Ariel made a deal with the sea witch, Ursula. In exchange for her voice, Ursula gave Ariel a potion that would turn her into a human for three days. If Ariel received a true love's kiss from the prince within that time, she would remain human; otherwise, she would turn back into a mermaid and belong to Ursula forever.


Ariel drank the potion and transformed into a human, but when the prince found her, he didn't recognize her without her voice. Despite Ariel's efforts to win his love, Ursula, disguised as a beautiful woman, used Ariel's voice to enchant the prince into believing she was his true love.


On the final day, Ariel's friends discovered Ursula's plot and revealed the truth. The prince, realizing his true feelings for Ariel, kissed her just in time. Ursula's plan was thwarted, and Ariel's voice was restored. She remained human, and she and the prince were married, living happily ever after.


The Sea King, seeing Ariel's happiness, blessed their union, and peace was restored to both the land and the sea.
