Cinderella


Once upon a time, in a faraway kingdom, there lived a kind and beautiful girl named Cinderella. After her mother passed away, her father remarried a cruel woman who had two equally cruel daughters. When Cinderella's father died, her stepmother and stepsisters made her their servant, forcing her to do all the household chores and sleep in the cold attic.



Despite her hardships, Cinderella remained gentle and hopeful. One day, the king announced a royal ball to find a bride for the prince. Every maiden in the kingdom was invited. Cinderella longed to attend, but her stepmother refused, giving her extra chores to ensure she couldn't go.


As Cinderella wept in despair, her Fairy Godmother appeared. With a wave of her magic wand, she transformed a pumpkin into a magnificent carriage, mice into horses, a rat into a coachman, and lizards into footmen. She turned Cinderella's rags into a stunning gown and glass slippers, but warned that the magic would end at midnight.


At the ball, Cinderella captivated the prince with her beauty and grace. They danced the night away, but as midnight approached, Cinderella had to leave in haste, accidentally losing one of her glass slippers.


The prince, determined to find the mysterious girl, searched the kingdom with the slipper. Every maiden tried it on, but it fit none. When Cinderella's turn came, the shoe fit perfectly. Her Fairy Godmother reappeared, revealing Cinderella in her beautiful gown.

Cinderella married the prince, and they lived happily ever after, while her stepmother and stepsisters were left to face the consequences of their cruelty. The kingdom rejoiced in the happiness of the kind and gentle Cinderella, who never lost hope even in the darkest times.
