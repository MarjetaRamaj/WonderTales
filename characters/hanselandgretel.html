Hansel and Gretel


Once upon a time, in a small cottage near a dark forest, lived a poor woodcutter with his two children, Hansel and Gretel, and their stepmother. Times were tough, and food was scarce. The stepmother, worried they would all starve, convinced the woodcutter to abandon the children in the forest.



One night, Hansel overheard their plan and gathered white pebbles. The next day, as they were led into the forest, Hansel dropped the pebbles along the way. When their parents left them, the pebbles shone in the moonlight, guiding Hansel and Gretel back home.


The stepmother was furious and the next day, she took them deeper into the forest. This time, Hansel used breadcrumbs to mark the path, but birds ate them. Lost and hungry, the children wandered until they found a house made of candy.


A kind old woman invited them inside, but she was actually a wicked witch. She locked Hansel in a cage to fatten him up and made Gretel her servant. Clever Gretel devised a plan. When the witch prepared to cook Hansel, Gretel pretended not to understand how to check the oven. As the witch demonstrated, Gretel pushed her in and shut the door, burning the witch to ashes.


Hansel and Gretel found treasure in the witch's house and filled their pockets. They retraced their steps and eventually found their way home.
